Rich Dad Poor Dad by Robert Kiyosaki

Get the Book on Amazon.

Core Principle: Financial Freedom comes from building wealth through assets that generate income (cashflow) rather than relying solely on a traditional job and salary.

    Key Lessons:
  • The Two Dads: The book uses the story of Kiyosaki's relationships with his biological father ("poor dad") and his friend's father (rich dad") to illustrate contrasting views on money and wealth creation.
  • Rat Race vs. Financial Freedom: Rich Dad Poor Dad criticizes the traditional "get a good job, work hard, save money" approach, arguing it keeps people trapped in a cycle of debt and limited financial options. The book emphasizes building assets that generate income (financial freedom).
  • The Cashflow Quadrant: Kiyosaki introduces the Cashflow Quadrant, dividing business models into four categories (Employee - E, Self-Employed - S, Business Owner - B, Investor - I) and highlights the B & I quadrants for wealth building through businesses and investments that generate passive income.
  • Financial Education: The book stresses the importance of financial literacy for navigating the modern economic landscape and making informed investment decisions.
