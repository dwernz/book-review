The Secrets of Successful Selling Habits by Zig Ziglar

Get the Book on Amazon.

Core Principle: Ziglar argues that selling is not just about transactions; it's about building relationships, understanding customer needs, and providing value. Success comes from developing positive habits, a winning attitude, and genuine customer focus.

    Key Lessons:
  • Developing a Positive Attitude: The book emphasizes the importance of maintaining a positive and enthusiastic outlook. Your belief in yourself and your product is contagious and fosters trust with potential customers.
  • The Power of Relationships: Building genuine relationships with customers is crucial. Ziglar encourages active listening, understanding customer needs, and providing solutions that benefit them.
  • Habit Stacking for Success: The book outlines 25 specific sales habits to cultivate.
    1. A sincere compliment is a good way to start a presentation.
    2. The best way to make a new sale is to make that prospect feel good about a previous purchase.
    3. A sales professional, if he really wants to be successful, will ask an awful lot of questions. That's the way you get information from people: ask them questions.
    4. They will give you pertinent information if you just ask. All you have to do is ask, and in most cases they will.
    5. Salespeople should be optimistic.
    6. To succeed in selling, you, the salesperson, must sit on both sides of the table. You have to think as a buyer, and you also have to think as a seller.
    7. If there's anything wrong, if there are any questions, if there are any objections, it is better to deal with them early in the presentation instead of later on as a rebuttal. This way you can sell on the offense and not on the defense.
    8. The prospect buys only when he believes and/or understands. When you write it out, his chance of believing and understanding is much greater.
    9. When you use the right voice inflection - I, the customer, have to make an important couple of decisions.
    10. Don't argue. Don't get defensive or try to justify the price at that point. Give the ball back to the prospect.
    11. Use the Abraham Lincoln approach. Represent both sides of the story.
    12. Defend the prospect's previous purchase, even if the prospect says ugly things about it. You rise above it.
    13. You need to find out if you're in the same ballpark or if you're in different ball games.
    14. At a major decision time, the prospect is temporarily insane. They are so excited or confused or puzzled, they don't know. At this point, the kind of person you are is infinitely more important than the kind of salesperson you are. Your integrity is so important because, you see, if the prospect doesn't trust you, he won't buy from you.
    15. The right kind of person in sales will train and study to develop professional sales skills.
    16. Communicate very important messages to prospects.
    17. If the customer isn't buying, you might need to do more selling.
    18. Don't Panic.
    19. Control your emotions.
    20. Get on the customer's side of the table.
    21. Use a very subtle version of the assumptive close.
    22. Tie down the details.
    23. Tie does the sale.
    24. If you're going to build a sales career, you have to keep in touch with your prospects.
    25. Greet the customer, be the first person they see.

  • Service is Key: Ziglar emphasizes that true salespeople prioritize exceptional customer service. This builds loyalty and encourages repeat business.
  • Embrace Technology: While written before the digital age's full impact, the book highlights the importance of using technology effectively to connect with customers, manage leads, and stay organized.
  • Overcoming Objections: The book provides strategies for gracefully handling customer objections and turning them into opportunities to showcase the value you offer.
  • Effective Communication: Developing strong communication skills is crucial for salespeople. Ziglar emphasizes clear and concise communication, as well as active listening to understand customer needs.
  • Time Management: Effective time management allows you to prioritize tasks, maximize productivity, and ensure you're dedicating time to high-value activities.
