Core Principle: Lasting change comes from focusing on small, incremental improvements in your daily habits.

    Key Lessons:
  • The 1% Rule: Small, consistent improvements (even 1% better each day) lead to significant results over time, thanks to the power of compounding.
  • Focus on Systems over Goals: Goals are about outcomes, while systems are the processes you follow to achieve those goals. Create a system that supports your desired habits.
  • Identity Change: Your habits shape your identity. By focusing on becoming the type of person who has good habits, positive change becomes natural.
  • The Habit Loop: Understand the four-step cycle of cue, craving, response, and reward that drives habits. Use this knowledge to design habits that stick.
  • Make Habits Obvious: Create a physical environment that cues desired behaviors and hides temptations.
  • Habit Stacking: Link new habits to existing routines to make them easier to adopt.
  • Temptation Bundling: Pair an undesired habit with a desired one to make the unwanted behavior less appealing.
  • The 2-Minute Rule: Lower the barrier to entry for new habits by making them incredibly easy to start (e.g., floss for 2 minutes).
  • The Goldilocks Rule: Find the "just right" challenge level for your habits to maximize motivation and avoid burnout.
